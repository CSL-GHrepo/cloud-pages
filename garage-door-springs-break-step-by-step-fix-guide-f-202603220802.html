Quick Signs Your Garage Door Springs Are Broken

Before jumping into repair, it’s crucial to correctly identify the problem. Springs are under extreme tension and controlling the door’s weight—missing this can cause injury or further damage.

  • Loud bang or snap: One of the most obvious signs is a sudden snapping sound when the spring breaks.
  • Door feels heavy or won’t open/close: If your garage door suddenly becomes hard to lift manually or won’t stay open, a spring is likely broken.
  • Visible gap in spring: Inspect visually (only from a safe distance); a break in the torsion spring coil is a clear indication.
  • Garage door tilts or moves unevenly: Broken springs can cause unbalanced movement or door misalignment.
According to industry data, around 20% of garage door repairs relate to springs annually, highlighting their critical role and failure rate. (Source: International Door Association)

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Fix Garage Door Springs Safely

Garage door springs fall into two main types: torsion springs and extension springs. Each requires slightly different attention but both involve tension that can be dangerous.

1. Assess and Prepare

  • Disconnect power to the automatic opener to prevent accidental activation.
  • Secure the door in the closed position with clamps or locking pliers to prevent movement during repair.
  • Gather appropriate tools, including winding bars (not screwdrivers), adjustable wrenches, safety glasses, and gloves.
  • If unfamiliar with mechanical repairs or lacking tools, strongly consider professional garage door spring replacement services.

2. Removing the Broken Spring

  • Identify the type of spring (torsion springs are mounted above the door on a metal shaft; extension springs run along the sides).
  • Use winding bars to carefully unwind torsion springs—this is the most dangerous step.
  • For extension springs, remove the safety cables first before unhooking the spring.
  • Remove all mounting hardware carefully and keep track of all parts for reassembly.

3. Installing Replacement Springs

  • Purchase springs matching the door’s specifications (weight, height, and type). Incorrect sizes can cause hazardous malfunction.
  • Attach replacement springs using the original mounting brackets and hardware.
  • For torsion springs, wind new springs precisely to factory torque specifications.

4. Adjusting Garage Door Spring Tension

  • Proper tension ensures smooth door operation and safety.
  • Use winding bars to increase or reduce tension incrementally, testing door balance frequently.
  • Door should stay open about 3-4 feet when lifted halfway manually.

5. Perform Cable and Spring Maintenance

  • Lubricate springs and cables with a silicone-based lubricant monthly to prevent rust and premature wear.
  • Inspect cables for frays or damage during and after repair—replace as necessary.
  • Test door operation manually before reconnecting the automatic opener.

Garage Door Spring Safety Tips Every Homeowner Should Know

Garage door springs hold significant tension and can cause serious injury or damage if mishandled. Professionals recommend the following safety precautions:

  • Never attempt DIY garage door spring repair without proper knowledge, tools, and safety precautions.
  • Always use the proper winding bars; screwdrivers and other substitutes can slip and cause injury.
  • Wear safety glasses and gloves during repair to protect against flying debris or snapped components.
  • Disconnect the garage door opener to prevent accidental activation during repair.
“Garage door springs are under tremendous pressure, and incorrect handling can lead to serious injury. Industry professionals recommend leaving spring repairs to trained technicians unless you have hands-on experience and proper tools,” says Mike Jensen, a certified garage door technician with over 15 years of service.

When to Call a Professional for Garage Door Spring Replacement

Even with proper guidance and tools, garage door spring repair can be risky. Homeowners should consider hiring a professional if:

  • They notice multiple broken springs or additional damage to cables and hardware.
  • The door is unusually heavy or unbalanced, indicating complex torsion spring issues.
  • They lack specialized winding bars or accessible replacement parts.
  • They live in regions with high humidity or extreme weather where garage door cable and spring maintenance is critical for longevity.

Professional technicians not only ensure safety but also provide warranty assurance and can spot underlying issues preventing future failures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are common signs of broken garage door springs?

Signs include a loud bang or snapping sound, difficulty opening or closing the door, uneven door movement, and visible gaps or breaks in the spring itself. If you notice any of these, it’s important to inspect the springs safely or contact a professional immediately.

Can I safely perform DIY garage door spring repair?

DIY repair is risky due to the high tension springs hold. Only attempt if you have the right tools like winding bars, safety equipment, and a thorough understanding of garage door spring tension adjustment. Otherwise, professional garage door spring replacement is strongly recommended.

How often should garage door cable and spring maintenance be performed?

Maintenance such as lubrication and visual inspections should be done every six months to a year. Regular maintenance prevents rust, reduces wear on cables and springs, and extends the door’s operational lifespan.

Why is garage door spring tension adjustment important?

Correct tension ensures the door operates smoothly and balances the door's weight. Improper tension can cause the door to slam shut or fail to open fully, potentially damaging the door or endangering users.
