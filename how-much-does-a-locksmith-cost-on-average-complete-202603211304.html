According to industry studies, the average locksmith charges between $50 and $150 for basic services such as lockouts and lock changes. Understanding Locksmith Service Rates: What Affects the Price?

Several factors influence locksmith pricing, including:

  • Type of Service: Lock change, lockout, rekeying, safe installation, or security system setup each have different rates.
  • Time of Service: Emergency calls after hours, weekends, or holidays often incur additional fees.
  • Location: Locksmith costs vary by region due to labor and living cost differences.
  • Complexity: High-security locks, electronic systems, and commercial-grade hardware are more expensive.
  • Urgency: Rapid response or mobile locksmith deployment can raise costs.

Residential Locksmith Pricing

For homeowners, locksmith costs are typically more affordable. The average lock change cost for a standard residential lock ranges between $60 and $120. Rekeying—changing the internal pins without replacing the hardware—is cheaper, usually $20 to $50 per lock.

Commercial Locksmith Charges

Businesses often face higher rates due to complex security needs. Installing commercial locks or access control systems can run between $120 and $400 per lock. Hourly rates for commercial locksmith services range from $75 to $150 depending on the skill level and equipment needed.

Car Lockout Cost and Automotive Locksmiths

Locked out of your car? Automotive locksmith services typically cost between $75 and $150 for a car lockout. The price increases for more sophisticated key fobs or transponder key programming, which can range between $150 and $300+.

Tips to Get the Best Value From Locksmith Services

  • Request a Written Estimate: Before work begins, ask for a clear quote covering parts, labor, and any emergency fees.
  • Check Credentials: Licensed, insured locksmiths offer better security and accountability.
  • Compare Local Rates: Prices fluctuate by region; researching nearby providers can save money.
  • Avoid Lockout Scams: Verify the company’s identity; unscrupulous providers may inflate emergency locksmith fees.
  • Plan Ahead: Scheduling non-emergency lock changes or rekeying during business hours lowers cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are typical locksmith hourly rates for service calls?

Locksmith hourly rates generally range from $50 to $100 per hour depending on the complexity of the job and location. Simple tasks may be charged with flat rates, but extensive security system installs or commercial jobs often use hourly pricing.

How much does an emergency locksmith usually charge?

Emergency locksmith fees can add $50 to $100 or more to a basic service, raising total costs to between $150 and $250. These fees apply for after-hours, weekend, or holiday calls when locksmith availability is limited.

What is the average cost to change locks at home?

The average lock change cost for residential properties is between $60 and $120. This includes standard deadbolt replacement. Rekeying locks—a less expensive option—often costs $20 to $50 per lock.

How much does it cost to get a key cut by a locksmith?

Key cutting prices vary between $3 and $10 for standard keys. Specialized automotive or high-security keys can cost significantly more, sometimes exceeding $100 depending on technology like transponders.
