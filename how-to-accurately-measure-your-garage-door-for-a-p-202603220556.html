Quick Answer: How to Measure for a New Garage Door

To measure your garage door accurately, start by measuring the width and height of the garage opening from inside the garage at three different points. Use the narrowest width and shortest height measurements to select your door size. Also, measure the side rooms on both sides and the headroom above the opening to ensure the door and installation hardware will fit properly.

Tools You’ll Need for Measuring Your Garage Door

  • Measuring tape (steel tape measure recommended)
  • Step ladder for headroom measurements
  • Notepad and pen or smartphone to record measurements
  • Helper (optional but recommended for safety and accuracy)

Step-by-Step Guide: Measuring Garage Door Dimensions Accurately

1. Measure the Door Width

From inside the garage, measure the width of the door opening from the inside edge of the left jamb to the inside edge of the right jamb. Take three measurements:

  • Top of the opening
  • Middle of the opening
  • Bottom of the opening

Record the narrowest width. This prevents ordering a door that is too large to fit.

2. Measure the Door Height

Measure the height from the floor slab or concrete floor to the inside top of the door opening at three points:

  • Left side
  • Center
  • Right side

Use the shortest height measurement to ensure proper fit, especially if floors or headers are uneven.

3. Determine the Headroom

Headroom is the distance from the inside top of the door opening to the lowest obstruction of the ceiling or garage overhead. This space is crucial for mounting the door tracks and springs. Measure the headroom along the entire width of the door opening for any irregularities.

4. Measure the Side Room

Side room is the distance from each side of the door opening to the nearest wall or obstruction inside the garage. Standard side room is typically a minimum of 3.5 inches, but some door systems may require more.

5. Check the Backroom

Backroom is the depth inside the garage from the garage door opening to the back wall. This measurement affects the type of door that can be installed, especially if opting for a traditional sectional versus a low headroom door.

Garage Door Replacement Considerations

When measuring for garage door replacement, keep in mind that new doors must fit the exact rough opening size with allowances for operational hardware. If the dimensions do not match the standard sizes, custom doors may be necessary.

Additionally, factoring in insulation and material thickness is important for proper garage door sizing and installation measurements. For example, some insulated doors are thicker and require slightly more clearance space.

Expert Tips for Accurate Garage Door Measurement

  • Always use a steel measuring tape for precision.
  • Measure twice, record thrice — double-check measurements to avoid mistakes.
  • Take photos of the garage door opening and existing door to assist professionals.
  • Measure from the inside of the garage, never outside from the driveway.
  • Account for the curvature or damage in the existing garage door frame, measuring actual opening dimensions rather than door panel size.
  • If unsure, consult with garage door professionals who can provide onsite measurements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I measure for a garage door replacement accurately?

To measure for garage door replacement, measure the width and height of the garage door opening inside the garage at three points each to find the narrowest width and shortest height. Don't forget to measure the side room and headroom for proper fitting of hardware and door operation.

What is the standard size for a garage door?

Standard single garage doors typically measure 8 or 9 feet wide and 7 or 8 feet high. Double doors are usually 16 feet wide by 7 or 8 feet high. However, exact sizes vary, so measuring your specific opening is necessary for proper garage door sizing.

Can I measure my garage door from the outside?

It’s best to measure garage door dimensions from inside the garage to get precise width, height, side room, and headroom measurements. Exterior measurements can be misleading due to trims, driveway slopes, or framing differences.

How important is headroom measurement in garage door installation?

Headroom measurement is critical because it determines the installation space available for the garage door tracks, springs, and opener. Insufficient headroom may require special low-clearance doors or alternative installation methods.
