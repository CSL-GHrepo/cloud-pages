Common Penalties When Breaking Copier Lease Contracts

Breaking a copier lease contract prematurely activates the lease agreement breach copier penalties designed to compensate the leasing company. Common penalties include:

  1. Early Termination Fees: Often calculated as a percentage of remaining payments or the full remaining balance. For example, a 50% to 100% charge of unpaid lease installments is common.
  2. Equipment Return Costs: Lessees may be responsible for shipping and/or refurbishment costs, especially if the equipment is not returned in good condition.
  3. Buyout Fees: An option to settle the lease early by paying a defined buyout cost, which may align with the equipment’s fair market or residual value.
  4. Additional Administrative Fees: Charges related to contract processing or re-leasing the equipment.

Here’s a real-world example: A mid-size firm leased a $15,000 copier over 36 months. Sixteen months in, they terminated due to downsizing. The early lease termination fees demanded included 50% of the remaining payments ($4,500), plus a $500 refurbishment charge — resulting in unexpected expenses totaling nearly one-third of the original contract value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are early lease termination fees in copier leasing?

Early lease termination fees are charges that the leasing company imposes when you break a copier lease contract before the agreed term ends. These fees typically cover the lessor’s lost revenue, the depreciated value of the equipment, and administrative costs. They can range from a percentage of the remaining lease payments to the full unpaid amount of the lease.

Can I buy out my copier lease to avoid cancellation charges?

Yes, many copier leases include a buyout option that allows you to pay a set amount, often called the residual or fair market value, to own the equipment outright and avoid ongoing payments and penalties. However, buyout costs can still be substantial, so compare these costs against potential penalties before deciding.

What happens if I breach a copier equipment lease agreement?

Breaching a copier equipment lease typically results in penalties such as early termination fees, equipment return obligations, and possible legal action if payments are not settled. The exact consequences depend on the specific terms outlined in your lease contract’s early termination clauses.

Are copier lease penalties the same everywhere?

No, copier lease penalties can vary based on the leasing company, contract terms, and regional laws. Some states have regulations limiting excessive penalties or providing consumer protections, so it’s important to understand local legal frameworks affecting copier lease cancellation charges.
